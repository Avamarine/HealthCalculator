BMI is used to assess the possibility of excess body fat but is not a calculation of body fat percentage.

Body Mass Index (BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a person’s weight divided by their height. Because there is a moderate correlation between BMI and body fat, it can be used as an estimating tool but is not a true diagnostic of the body’s fat ratio nor the health of an individual.

To determine if a high BMI is a health risk, you would need to seek a further assessment from a qualified healthcare professional.

For adults 20 years and older, BMI is interpreted using standard weight status categories. These categories are the same for men and women of all body types and ages.
